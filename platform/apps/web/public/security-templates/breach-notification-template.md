## What You Can Do

We recommend you take the following steps to protect yourself:

### Monitor Your Accounts

- Review your bank and credit card statements for unauthorized activity
- Check your credit reports at www.annualcreditreport.com (free)
- Consider placing a fraud alert on your credit file

### Fraud Alert Information

Contact any one of the three credit bureaus to place a free fraud alert:

- **Equifax:** 1-800-525-6285 | www.equifax.com
- **Experian:** 1-888-397-3742 | www.experian.com
- **TransUnion:** 1-800-680-7289 | www.transunion.com

### Credit Freeze

You may also consider placing a credit freeze, which prevents new accounts from being opened in your name. Contact each bureau:

- Equifax: 1-800-349-9960
- Experian: 1-888-397-3742
- TransUnion: 1-888-909-8872

### Report Suspicious Activity

- Report identity theft to the FTC at www.identitytheft.gov
- File a police report if you become a victim of identity theft
- Contact your financial institutions if you notice unauthorized charges
