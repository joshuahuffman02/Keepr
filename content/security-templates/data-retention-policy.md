## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how long [Campground Name] retains different types of data and when that data must be securely deleted. Proper data retention protects customer privacy, reduces liability, and ensures compliance with applicable laws.

---

## 2. Data Categories and Retention Periods

### 2.1 Customer Reservation Data

| Data Type                   | Retention Period             | Reason                       |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Reservation records         | 7 years after checkout       | Tax/accounting requirements  |
| Guest contact information   | 3 years after last stay      | Marketing and re-booking     |
| Payment transaction records | 7 years                      | Financial audit requirements |
| Credit card numbers         | Do not store (use processor) | PCI-DSS compliance           |

### 2.2 Employee Data

| Data Type                | Retention Period                                                    | Reason                 |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Current employee records | Duration of employment                                              | Operational necessity  |
| Former employee records  | 7 years after separation                                            | Legal/tax requirements |
| Payroll records          | 7 years                                                             | IRS requirements       |
| I-9 forms                | 3 years after hire or 1 year after termination (whichever is later) | USCIS requirements     |

### 2.3 Operational Data

| Data Type               | Retention Period | Reason                     |
| ----------------------- | ---------------- | -------------------------- |
| Security camera footage | 30-90 days       | Incident investigation     |
| System access logs      | 1 year           | Security monitoring        |
| Incident reports        | 7 years          | Legal liability            |
| Maintenance records     | 5 years          | Equipment warranty/history |

### 2.4 Marketing Data

| Data Type              | Retention Period           | Reason               |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Email subscriber lists | Until unsubscribe + 1 year | Marketing compliance |
| Website analytics      | 2 years                    | Business analysis    |
| Survey responses       | 2 years                    | Service improvement  |

## 3. Deletion Procedures

### 3.1 Electronic Data

- [ ] Delete files using secure deletion software (not just trash)
- [ ] Remove from all backup systems within 30 days
- [ ] Confirm deletion with IT/system administrator
- [ ] Log deletion date and responsible party

### 3.2 Paper Documents

- [ ] Cross-cut shred all documents containing personal information
- [ ] Use a certified shredding service for large volumes
- [ ] Never dispose of unshredded documents in regular trash

### 3.3 Equipment Disposal

- [ ] Wipe all hard drives before disposal/recycling
- [ ] Factory reset mobile devices
- [ ] Destroy storage media that cannot be wiped
- [ ] Obtain certificate of destruction from disposal vendor

---

## 4. Exceptions

Data may be retained longer than specified if:

1. Required for ongoing legal proceedings
2. Subject to a litigation hold
3. Required by regulatory investigation
4. Specifically requested by customer for service continuity

All exceptions must be documented with:

- Reason for extended retention
- New expected deletion date
- Approval by [Title/Role]

---

## 5. Customer Data Requests

When customers request deletion of their data:

1. Verify customer identity
2. Check for legal/business reasons requiring retention
3. Delete data within 30 days if no exceptions apply
4. Confirm deletion to customer in writing
5. Document the request and action taken

---

## 6. Annual Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by [Title/Role] to ensure:

- Retention periods comply with current laws
- Deletion procedures are being followed
- No unnecessary data is being stored
