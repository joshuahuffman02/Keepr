## Introduction

If you're not accepting online payments, you're creating friction for guests and likely losing bookings. Here's how to set up payment processing correctly.

---

## Payment Processing Basics

### How It Works

1. Guest enters payment information
2. Payment processor verifies card
3. Funds are authorized (held)
4. Transaction completes at checkout
5. Funds deposit to your account (1-3 days)

### Key Terms

- **Payment gateway:** The technology processing payments
- **Merchant account:** Where funds are deposited
- **Processing fee:** Cost per transaction (typically 2.5-3%)
- **PCI compliance:** Security standards requirement

---

## What to Accept

### Credit/Debit Cards

- Visa, Mastercard (essential)
- American Express, Discover (recommended)
- Debit cards (usually same processing)

### Digital Wallets

- Apple Pay
- Google Pay
- Increasingly expected by guests

### Optional

- ACH/bank transfer (for extended stays)
- Cash (in-person only)
- Checks (higher risk)

---

## Choosing a Processor

### Key Factors

- Processing fee percentage
- Per-transaction fee
- Monthly fees
- Integration with your software
- Payout timing
- Customer support

### Integrated vs. Standalone

**Integrated (recommended):**

- Works within your reservation software
- Automatic payment tracking
- Single system to manage

**Standalone:**

- Separate terminal or account
- Manual reconciliation needed
- More work, more errors

---

## Security Requirements

### PCI Compliance

- Required for credit card processing
- Your software should handle most requirements
- Never store full card numbers
- Use secure, encrypted connections

### Best Practices

- Use trusted payment processors
- Keep software updated
- Train staff on security
- Monitor for suspicious activity

---

## Handling Common Situations

**Deposits:** Charge at booking, apply to final bill
**Balances due:** Collect before or at check-in
**Refunds:** Process through same system
**Chargebacks:** Document everything, respond promptly

---

## Key Takeaways

- Online payments are expected by guests
- Integrated processing reduces errors
- Security and PCI compliance are required
- Fees are a cost of doing business
